# Act 1: Our Past

You know how they say every great story starts with someone having a brilliant idea? Yeah, this isn't that story.
You ever look back at your life choices and think, "Damn, that was either really brave or really stupid"? That's basically our origin story.

## CHAPTER 1: Audacity: A Love Story between Dreams & Delusion
"We had the kind of education that teaches you mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell but leaves you clueless about taxes, filmmaking, or the art of professional bullshitting. But hey, who needs experience when you have audacity, right? And because nobody told us we couldn't. Or maybe they did, and we weren't listening. You know that moment in horror movies where everyone's screaming "Don't go in there!" at the screen? We were the idiots running straight into the haunted house of filmmaking, armed with nothing but audacity and a borrowed camera. Basically, the kind of confidence you can only get from having absolutely no idea what you're doing.

I know what you're thinking – "How bad could it be?"
See, most people graduate high school and think about college. We graduated and thought, "Hey, you know what sounds fun? Let's make films!" With no equipment. No connections. And definitely no clue. The kind of decision that makes your parents question their genetic contribution to your intelligence.

## CHAPTER 2: A Symphony of Spectacular Fuck-ups (Our greatest hits album, featuring zero hits)
We kept failing up. Like, aggressively up. The kind of failing where you're not sure if you're getting better or just getting better at hiding the mistakes. You know what's funnier than failing? Failing with an audience. Every weekend we'd tell our parents "This is it!" By Monday, we were experts in creative excuses. "The sun wasn't artistic enough" was actually used once. We had more "final attempts" than a toxic relationship has "last chances." We had so many failed attempts at IFP's 50-hour challenge, we could've made a film about not making films. Spoiler: We probably would've failed at that too.

Friends came and went – some couldn't handle the chaos, others wanted safer paths. We were learning the hardest lesson in creativity: not everyone who starts the journey makes it to the wyrd part. Turns out, "Trust me, this will work" isn't as convincing the seventh time around. But it did work.

## CHAPTER 3: The Universe Blinked First (Turns out stubbornness is a business strategy)
"All The Best" happened. Not because we finally got good – oh no, that would be too logical. We developed immunity to embarrassment. We just failed so many times we ran out of fucks to give about doing it "right." Two survivors of our own amateur hour, making something that didn't completely suck. 5,000 YouTube views. To put that in perspective, that's about 4,990 more views than our moms could generate with fake accounts.

## CHAPTER 4: When the Ball Started Rolling (And We Couldn't Stop It)
Then came what I like to call our "Yes, And?" era. You know how they say desperation is the mother of invention? Well, we were like a whole orphanage of invention. "Can you meme?" Sure! "Design websites?" Why not! "Build a rocket to Mars?" Let me watch a YouTube tutorial real quick. We were basically a startup inside a film school inside a creative agency. We were taking up any and every creative gig that could fund our film addiction.

Then came Movifi's 50-hour challenge. Now, this is where it gets good. We're in this village that Google Maps probably lists as "Loading...". We're running on cups of chai and poor life choices, probably hallucinating from sleep deprivation. You know how some people say constraints breed creativity? Try making a film in 50 hours with a crew running on dreams and deadline panic. But here's the thing – sometimes when you don't have time to doubt yourself, magic happens.

We win Best Film and Best Director. The jury must've been as sleep-deprived as we were. Or maybe – just maybe – being too tired to overthink actually makes you better at art.
"Kakbagodaa" - Our little film that could, did. We travelled with the film from the biggest cities to the remotest village in multiple film festivals. Some friends who'd jumped ship earlier? They saw what we were building and swam back.

Then Covid hit. Everyone's making banana bread like it's gonna save humanity. Meanwhile, what are WE doing? Oh, just casually MAKING FILMS IN A PANDEMIC. If that sounds insane, that's because it absolutely fucking was. While people are learning to unmute on Zoom, we're out here with cameras like "Yeah, this seems like the perfect time to double down on bad decisions!" because apparently, our survival instinct was as broken as our work-life balance.

And here's the beautiful part - you know what happens? After years of making IFP our personal highlight reel of failures - after so many attempts they should've named a rejection letter after us - we finally WIN. With "Chalte Rahiye Janaab." During a PANDEMIC.
We had to prove it wasn't a fluke. Made "Jama" and "Peekaboo" back-to-back after it.

## CHAPTER 5: The Bombae Chronicles
Akarsh Khurana (yes, @akvarious) spotted us at a festival. Ghosted us and then pulled a reverse Uno card and came back with "Jugaadistan" for Lionsgate Play. Mumbai came calling. That city that eats dreams and shits reality shows.

Now, most people would take this as their big break. We took it as permission to be sleep deprived in a more expensive city. Our first big set experience. Living the dream! You know, that dream where you're constantly exhausted but somehow still functioning? That one.
Meanwhile, we're still winning at IFP with "Stand Out" in 2021 & "Kachcha Papitaa" in 2022 because apparently, we also needed to test if we could be in two places at once.

## CHAPTER 6: Delhi Round 2: It's Personal this time! (How to steal your future back)
After running around with Lionsgate, Netflix, Zee5 - living that sweet studio life - we head back to Delhi. And here's the gut punch - some of the crew comes back with us, rides The Urban Desi wave 2.0, then bounces. It stung. Like watching your kids leave for college, except you're barely older than college age yourself and your kids are actually your friends who you've emotionally adopted.

## CHAPTER 7: Turns Out We Were Right For All The Wrong Reasons (Spoiler: Maybe we did all along)
Here's the wyrd thing - They say every great story starts with someone crazy enough to try something that shouldn't work. Well, this is a story about being crazy enough to keep trying even after proving multiple times that it definitely doesn't work. And here's the thing about banging your head against a wall - sometimes the wall gives up before you do. And when it breaks – it's worth every sleepless night, every failed attempt, every friend who thought we were insane.

Because in the end, we didn't just make films. We made proof that sometimes, the best talent you can have is the inability to take no for an answer. Even when that 'no' is coming from reality itself. We convinced an entire industry we knew what we were doing. And somewhere between our third IFP win and people actually returning our calls, we realized - holy shit, maybe we do! Some of the best people you'll ever meet are the ones crazy enough to believe in your bullshit.

People ask me if we'd do it differently. Hell NO!
Because here's the thing about being stupid enough to chase your dreams – sometimes you catch them, sometimes they catch you, but the chase? That's where the story is.
And if there's one thing we're good at, it's making one hell of a story.

That's The Urban Desi story. Not 13 short films, festival wins, some adverts and fancy studio projects - though yeah, we did that too. It's the story of failing upwards so spectacularly that success finally went "Damn, I need to get me some of that. Every success story reaches a point where evolution isn't just an option—it's an imperative. While TUD had carved its niche in independent filmmaking, the industry was calling for something more comprehensive, more ambitious, more wyrdr.

Enter Wyrd Media Labs.
